Intro

My experience as a Thermal Engineer in NASA's L'SPACE Mission Concept Academy allowed me to design a spacecraft's thermal subsystem, applying both creativity and technical expertise. Through coursework in areas like heat transfer and machine design, I've built a solid academic foundation that fuels my passion for innovative solutions—enabling me to tackle complex engineering challenges where theory meets real-world application.

I am passionate about all facets of mechanical engineering, extending beyond thermal system design. I have also gained valuable experience in robotics and automation through a variety of hands-on projects. By working on robotic systems and automated solutions, I've developed a versatile engineering skill set that bridges mechanical design, thermal analysis, control systems, and robotics. This multidisciplinary approach allows me to tackle complex challenges and across multiple domains of engineering.

My Projects (Page 1 of 6)

Throughout my four years at Stony Brook University, I have endeavored in various projects in order to hone my skills. These projects vary from personal projects to class projects. Here are some of them:

Autonomous Valve Tester

Senior design project in collaboration with Belimo Aircontrols, automating the critical angle test for ball valves.

Youtube link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ApS5geGOWE&t=136s

  • Designed and fabricated a custom fixture to securely hold various sizes of ball valves during the automated testing process.
  • Developed a LabVIEW program to control the actuator, collect data from the encoder and flowmeter, and log results for analysis.
  • Integrated safety features, including emergency stops and operational interlocks, to ensure safe operation of the automated system.
  • Successfully demonstrated the system's capability to reduce test time by 75% compared to manual methods, while improving accuracy and repeatability.

NASA L’SPACE MCA PDR

Participated as a thermal engineer in NASA's L’SPACE Mission Concept Academy, supporting thermal system design for a robotic rover.

Youtube link for Preliminary Design Review Presentation: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huHxGpcssT0

  • Supported rover thermal system design by researching similar mission architectures, performing subsystem trade studies, and creating CAD models using Siemens NX.
  • Engaged with NASA scientists and engineers through weekly lectures and collaborative project work, developing technical and teamwork skills for aerospace engineering.

Digital PID Controller

Implemented a digital PID controller for a turntable speed control system, meeting strict performance criteria.

Youtube link: https://youtube.com/shorts/06u0TNoQ8cU?feature=share

  • Tuned the system to achieve a percent overshoot of less than 10%, settling time less than 500 ms, and rise time less than 200 ms.
  • Utilized custom LabView software for real-time data acquisition and tuning, alongside MATLAB for theoretical simulations and analysis.

Hot Water Dispenser

Conceptual design and CAD modeling of an in-line water heater for instant hot water delivery at the kitchen sink.

Youtube link: https://youtu.be/bsZSv6ZIcn8

  • Developed 3D CAD models via Autodesk Inventor for the entire water heater system, incorporating market and instrument research to inform the design.
  • Created a detailed, costed bill of materials for the system, supporting efficient sourcing and project planning.
